Configure general settings

This topic describes how to configure service settings to optimize the , features and functionality for your organization. These settings are available in the Settings > Service configuration page.

Changing these settings may require updates to other configurations in your environment, such as in policies, and so on.

We strongly recommend making these changes during a scheduled maintenance window.

Assign permissions to users

  • Direct assignment (default) - When a user signs in to the console, they receive permissions based on the role that they select, and the session duration is determined by the policy that grants access through that role.

    • Allow users to connect with multiple roles - Users can sign in to Azure with up to five roles simultaneously. Each role creates a separate session that lasts for the duration defined in the role's access policy.

      Multi-role selection is supported at the subscription, resource group, and resource levels only.

  • Group-based assignment - Permissions are assigned at the group level. When a user signs in to the console, they are assigned to a group and inherit the permissions granted to that group. The duration of the session is determined based on the maximum session length of the roles.

If you switch from direct assignment to group-based assignment, you have to recreate your policies in order for them to work properly.

Workspace scope

To enhance performance and manage workspace access more precisely, select the Azure scope levels you want to work with in :

  • Subscriptions (Default) - Management groups and subscriptions only

  • Resource groups - Management groups, subscriptions, and resource groups, but not resources

  • All - All scope levels: Management groups, subscriptions, resource groups, and resources

  • Reducing the scope may affect existing access policies and user access to Azure via

  • We recommend changing this setting during a scheduled maintenance window.

Assign permissions to users

Organizations can configure the method of assigning permissions when an policy is applied to a user.

Google Cloud quotas and limits on requests per minute and required resources may affect how many requests for assigning permissions can be made within a specific time frame. For more information, see the Google Cloud docs.

The method you set applies to all the workspace levels in your Google Cloud environment.

  • Direct assignment (default) - When a user signs in to the console, they receive permissions based on the role that they select, and the session duration is determined by the policy that grants access through that role.

    • Allow users to connect with multiple roles- Users can sign in with up to 5 roles simultaneously. Each selected role creates a separate session. The session lasts for the duration defined in the access policy for that role.

  • Group-based assignment - Permissions are assigned at the group level. When a user signs in to the console, they are assigned to a group and inherit the permissions granted to that group. The duration of the session is determined based on the maximum session length of the roles.

If you switch from direct assignment to group-based assignment, you have to recreate your policies in order for them to work properly.
